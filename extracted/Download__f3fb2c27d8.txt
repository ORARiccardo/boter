Subject matter:

Exchange of views on the potential contribution of fusion
technologies to the future production of medical radioisotopes in
the EU

Main points
raised and
positions
expressed:

Conclusions:

•

KFT Konzepte presented its activities, as a private
initiative, on the fusion-based Volumetric Neutron Source
(VNS) technology, building on expertise developed within
EUROfusion. VNS is considered by KFT Konzepte as a
testing facility for different fusion applications (tritium
breeding) and materials.

•

The company outlined a Radioisotope Generation System
(RIGs) concept for integrating radioisotope production
into a VNS facility. Preliminary studies at the Technical
University of Munich indicate a significant production
potential for different medical radioisotopes.

•

The discussion covered the technical maturity of the
concept, possible applications and timelines, regulatory
aspects and the potential role of such technologies within
the broader European radioisotope ecosystem.

•

The exchange also highlighted KFT Konzepte’s
proposal to leverage a future VNS fusion research
infrastructure for medical radioisotope production,
creating potential synergies with the European
radioisotope production ecosystem.

The Commission took note of the concept presented and, given
the early stage of development, encouraged KFT Konzepte to
continue engagement with the relevant research services, as
well as with the radioisotope production community.
