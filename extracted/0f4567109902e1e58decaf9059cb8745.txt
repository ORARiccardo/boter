Subject matter:

Exchange of views on developments in AI in payments, including
fraud and risk management issues, the rise of agentic commerce
and its interaction with the Payment Services Regulation (PSR),
the Digital Services Act (DSA) and the AI Act.

MasterCard: AI presents both opportunities and challenges in
the payments sector. The company works with AI labs to
monitor technological developments, which feed into internal
risk management processes, including in relation to
acquisitions. Managing innovation alongside risk remains an
important issue.
MasterCard: on AI alignment in financial services,
MasterCard indicated that it has made substantial investments
in security infrastructure and plans to continue doing so.
Fraud prevention remains a major priority across the
financial sector, particularly as AI creates new opportunities
for criminal misuse. Discussions with relevant stakeholders
are ongoing to help protect the wider ecosystem.
Renate Nikolay: Consumer protection issues linked to AIenabled commerce could potentially be addressed in the
Digital Fairness Act, while remaining consistent with
enforcement under the Digital Services Act.
MasterCard: characterised agentic AI as a further step in the
evolution of digital commerce. It stressed the importance of
preserving existing consumer protections, such as dispute

resolution mechanisms and anti-money laundering
requirements.

Conclusions:

MasterCard: sees AI as both promising and risky. It is engaging
with leading AI labs and investing strongly in AI for security and
fraud prevention.
The company views AI-driven commerce as a natural next step
and wants existing consumer safeguards to continue to apply.
More broadly, it called for a balanced EU approach that
considers both opportunities and risks, and for caution before
introducing additional regulation.
